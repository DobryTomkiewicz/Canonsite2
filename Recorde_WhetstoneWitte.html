The Whetstone of Witte

by Robert Recorde

Commentary

The most significant of Recorde's work. A sequel to his introduction to arithmetic, this volume is the first to use the equals sign, which happens at the very end, and is drawn much longer than the normal =. Before this, mathematicians such as Fibonacci could only write expressions as "2 and 2 make 4" (This is how he wrote some basic equations in the Liber Abaci).
