Collection

by Pappus

Commentary

One of the most significant extant mathematical works. It’s a shame that it’s nearly impossible to find in English. Even at the UPenn library, I could only find volumes 4 and 7. That search is one of the main reasons I’ve started this page, as a work this significant needs more eyes on it. Newton mentions Pappus in the opening sentence to his Principia. I believe the only reason there is not worldwide attention on this is due to the lack of a clear throughline in this era of intellectual history. Upon investigation, such a throughline clearly exists, but it is hard to find documentation of it.

Translations

  • A number of the volumes, except volumes 2, 4, and 7, are translated and released online for free by John B. Little at College of the Holy Cross.
