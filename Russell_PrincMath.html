principia mathematica

by russell and whitehead

Commentary

This work, along with its accompanying volume, The Principles of Mathematics, is probably worth reading for anyone interested in doing mathematics. While its intentions are vast in scope, it is more interesting to think of it as a series of essays about logic and proof writing. I don’t think it’s necessary to follow every symbolic laden hieroglyphics portion in the latter chunks to accomplish this purpose, but I feel spending some time with it can help give insight not only into modern mathematics, but also an alternative perception on what the ancient Greek mathematicians were doing.
