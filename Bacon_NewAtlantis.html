New Atlantis

by Bacon

Commentary

Anyone who claims Bacon is secretly Shakespeare has not read this frankly amateurish attempt at fiction. Famous for its speculations about a scientific society, these speculations only occur in the last three or so pages of this unfinished novella. The majority of this work is some of the sleepiest utopian fiction available. For most of this work, the only thing we know about this New Atlantis is that the people are all very polite and all of their problems are solved because they are all very Christian. Even what type of Christian is unclear. At one point a character looks at a bookshelf the New Atlantians have and remark in surprise about how many Bibles they have. It’s philosophically empty.
